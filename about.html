--- layout: default title: About group: "in_local_navigation" order: 2 --- # About IMEx The IMEx consortium is an international collaboration between a group of major public interaction data providers who have agreed to share curation effort and: * Develop and work to a single set of curation rules when capturing data from both directly deposited interaction data or from publications in peer-reviewed journals * Capture full details of an interaction in a “deep” curation model * Perform a complete curation of all protein-protein interactions experimentally demonstrated within a publication * Make these interaction available in a single search interface on a common website * Provide the data in standards compliant download formats * Make all IMEx records freely accessible under the Creative Commons Attribution License ### IMEx defines three types of membership: 1. Active: IMEx partner commits to producing relevant numbers of records curated to IMEx standard and providing these via a PSICQUIC service. 1. Observer: Prospective IMEx consortium member. 1. Inactive: former IMEx partner who contributed to the establishment of the IMEx curation rules and/or have contributed data to the IMEx dataset. ### IMEx partners: * DIP ([http://dip.doe-mbi.ucla.edu](http://dip.doe-mbi.ucla.edu/)) (Active) * HPIDB ([http://www.agbase.msstate.edu/hpi/main.html](http://www.agbase.msstate.edu/hpi/main.html)) (Active) * IntAct ([www.ebi.ac.uk/intact](http://www.ebi.ac.uk/intact)) (Active) * MBInfo ([http://www.mechanobio.info/](http://www.mechanobio.info/)) (Active) * MINT ([http://mint.bio.uniroma2.it/mint](http://mint.bio.uniroma2.it/mint/)) (Active) * MatrixDB ([http://matrixdb.ibcp.fr/](http://matrixdb.ibcp.fr/)) (Active) * Molecular Connections ([http://www.molecularconnections.com](http://www.molecularconnections.com "http://www.molecularconnections.com")) (Active) * I2D ([http://ophid.utoronto.ca/](http://ophid.utoronto.ca/ "http://ophid.utoronto.ca/")) (Active) * InnateDB ([www.innatedb.com](http://www.innatedb.com/)) (Active) * UCL-BHF group, UCL London ([http://www.ucl.ac.uk/functional-gene-annotation/cardiovascular](http://www.ucl.ac.uk/functional-gene-annotation/cardiovascular)) (Active) * UniProt Swiss-Prot group, SIB ([http://web.expasy.org/groups/swissprot/](http://web.expasy.org/groups/swissprot/)) (Active) * EMBL-EBI ([http://www.ebi.ac.uk/services/proteins](http://www.ebi.ac.uk/services/proteins)) (Active) * BioGRID ([http://www.thebiogrid.org/](http://www.thebiogrid.org/)) (Observer) * PrimesDB ([http://primesdb.org/](http://primesdb.org/)) (Observer) * MPact ([http://mips.gsf.de/genre/proj/mpact](http://mips.gsf.de/genre/proj/mpact)) (Inactive) * BIND ([www.blueprint.org](http://www.blueprint.org/)) (Inactive) * MPIDB ([http://www.jcvi.org/mpidb](http://www.jcvi.org/mpidb)) (Inactive) The IMEx consortium is open to the participation of additional partners. ### Data Availability

All IMEx Consortium data is centralised in the IntAct database at the EMBL-EBI. Member databases curate into IntAct, then make some or all of the data available at their own websites. Currently, the entire IMEx dataset can be accessed via the ([//www.ebi.ac.uk/intact](IntAct website)) (Active) and the ([//mentha.uniroma2.it/](metha web portal)) (Active). Biological domain-specific resources such as MatrixDB and HPIDB, only import specific subsets of data back into their own resources.

Data can be searched on the IMEx website ([IMEx website](//www.imexconsortium.org/home)) and downloaded in PSI-MI XML2.5 or MITAB format, enabling users to visualize the data in a wide range of tools such as Cytoscape and the R Bioconductor software suite. Species-specific PSI-MI XML2.5 and 3.0 and MITAB2.7 files can currently be accessed on ([//www.ebi.ac.uk/intact/downloads](www.ebi.ac.uk/intact/downloads)) (Active). Please be warned that this site contains legacy non-IMEx data from the DIP, IntAct, MINT and UniProtKB curation teams – a specific IMEx ftp site will be established later. All datasets will automatically increase with every release of the IntAct database.
